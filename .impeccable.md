# Design Context

## Users
**Both residential and commercial clients** in the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex seeking electrical services. They visit the website to:
- Evaluate credibility and professionalism
- Check services and pricing
- Book appointments
- Get quotes
- Review portfolio and testimonials

## Brand Personality
**Luxurious, Professional, Premium**

The brand conveys:
- Sophistication through meticulous attention to detail
- Reliability through licensed, insured expertise
- Excellence through premium service delivery
- Trust through transparent pricing and clear communication

## Aesthetic Direction
**Dark mode luxury - refined, premium, sophisticated**

Visual tone: Dark, elegant, with gold accents. High-contrast typography, sophisticated spacing, intentional animations. Think premium luxury brands, not utilitarian contractor sites.

Current implementation uses:
- **Colors**: Pure black (#000) as base with rich black (oklch(12% 0.005 280)) and gold accents (oklch(0.75 0.15 85))
- **Typography**: Space Grotesk (display) + Outfit (body) - refined, technical sophistication
- **Spacing**: 4pt base grid system with semantic tokens
- **Motion**: Exponential easing (ease-out-quart/quint/expo) for natural deceleration

## Design Principles

1. **Intentional Detail**: Every element exists for a reason. No decoration for decoration's sake. Gold accents highlight calls-to-action and important information.

2. **Sophisticated Restraint**: Dark backgrounds with precise typography. Generous white space (dark space) creates breathing room. Typography carries visual weight, not effects.

3. **Premium Interactions**: Smooth, purposeful animations. No jarring or gratuitous motion. Transitions feel expensive, not cheap.

4. **Technical Precision**: Clean, crisp execution. Proper spacing, perfect alignment, pixel-perfect implementation. Typography is the hero.

5. **Accessibility First**: High contrast ratios (WCAG AA/AAA), keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Premium shouldn't mean exclusive.

## Anti-References

**Generic contractor websites** - Avoid:
- Template/SaaS-looking patterns
- Overuse of cards/borders
- Generic contractor aesthetic (orange/blue color schemes)
- Crowded layouts
- Poor typography hierarchy
- Stock photos that feel fake
- Generic testimonials with no photos

**This should feel like a premium brand, not a service provider directory.**
